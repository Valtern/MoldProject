P001 L015: abstract
P001 L016: A reliable prediction of mould risk in buildings is important to ensure a healthy environment and to
P001 L017: avoid social and economical damage. Whereas previously the temperature ratio was often used to
P001 L018: minimize the mould risk, nowadays e more advanced e mould prediction models can be found (e.g.
P001 L019: isopleth systems, biohygrothermal model, ESP-r mould prediction model, empirical VTT model). These
P001 L020: models include the main in ﬂuencing factors for mould growth: the surface temperature and relative
P001 L021: humidity. However, they are based on either experiments or assumptions and some of them neglect
P001 L022: a third important in ﬂuencing factor: the exposure time.
P001 L023: The current paper gives an overview of the different existing models and analyses the impact of the
P001 L024: mould prediction model on the mould risk evaluation. To do so, the existing mould prediction models are
P001 L025: used to predict the mould risk for different temperature and relative humidity courses. The mould risk,
P001 L026: the time until mould growth starts and the mould intensity according to the existing prediction models
P001 L027: are compared. Based on the obtained results, the in ﬂuence of simpli ﬁcations or shortcomings in the
P001 L028: mould prediction models is discussed.

P001 L030: 1. Introduction
P001 L031: Mould spores are widely disseminated in the environment. Since
P001 L032: mould fungi enable the decomposition and recycling of dead organic
P001 L033: material, fungi are necessary in live. In buildings however, they can
P001 L034: deteriorate the interior ﬁnishing, which results in economical and
P001 L035: social damage. Furthermore, mould spores can include a risk for the
P001 L036: occupant’s health due to the settling and spreading of pathogens
P001 L037: [1e7], especially in cases of atopic individuals [8] or dangerous
P001 L038: mould species as Aspergillus Fumigatus or Stachybotris [9,10].
P001 L039: Consequently, attention should be paid to correct application of all
P001 L040: means that allow minimization of mould risk in buildings.
P001 L041: Before the 1970s, non-insulated buildings and severe thermal
P001 L042: bridges resulted in high energy losses and in many mould cases.
P001 L043: During the ﬁrst energy crisis, a growing awareness of the need to
P001 L044: improve buildings energy performance was developed. This resul-
P001 L045: ted in a general‘sealing action’, in which air leaks were minimized as
P001 L046: much as possible. Few attention was however paid to the adaptation
P001 L047: of the ventilation strategy, so an increased humidity level and
P001 L048: increased mould growth risk was obtained. Furthermore, with the
P001 L049: years more and more single glazed windows have been replaced by
P001 L050: new, very tight double glazed windows. Latter renovation technique
P001 L051: results not only in an increased air tightness but may also shift the
P001 L052: lowest surface temperature from the windows to the outside walls.
P001 L053: Consequently, a free detection system based on visible condensation
P001 L054: on the windows will be lost. Another renovation technique which
P001 L055: introduces an increased mould risk is the addition of interior insu-
P001 L056: lation, since thermal bridges are difﬁcult to avoid. Though, also new
P001 L057: buildings can deal with mould problems due to for example built-in
P001 L058: moisture, bad construction details inducing moisture damage or
P001 L059: a high moisture load in combination with an unsuitable ventilation
P001 L060: strategy [1 1,12].
P001 L061: First step in the avoidance of mould attack is the knowledge of
P001 L062: the boundary conditions under which moulds grow. Cardinal
P001 L063: inﬂuencing factors in the mould growth process are temperature,
P001 L064: moisture, substrate and exposure time[8,13,14,15]. The critical value
P001 L065: for these parameters can however differ for each mould species.
P001 L066: Minor in ﬂuencing factors are spore availability, oxygen, pH, light,
P001 L067: roughness of the surface, salt content of the substrate, etc. [13,14].
P001 L068: To evaluate the mould risk at building envelopes, different
P001 L069: design rules and prediction models can be found in literature (e.g.
P001 L070: temperature ratio, isopleth systems, biohygrothermal model, ESP-r
P001 L071: mould prediction model, empirical VTT-model, etc.). Each of these
P001 L072: models includes however different simpliﬁ cations or is based on
P001 L073: different assumptions. Consequently, to obtain a reliable indication
P001 L074: of the mould risk, the awareness of a potential in ﬂuence of the
P001 L075: model is required.
P001 L076: This paper compares the impact of the different existing mould
P001 L077: prediction models on the obtained mould risk conclusions. In
P001 L078: Section 2 the different existing mould prediction models are

P002 L007: 2. Mould prediction models
P002 L008: 2.1. IEA-Annex 14
P002 L009: To evaluate mould risk, the IEA-Annex 14 [8,16] proposes
P002 L010: a surface relative humidity threshold dependent on the elapsed
P002 L011: time. This relative humidity threshold is de ﬁned based on the
P002 L012: lowest isopleth for Aspergillus versicolor on agar, resulting in
P002 L013: a relative humidity threshold equal to 80%, 89% or 100% for
P002 L014: respectively an exposure time of 1 month, 1 week and 1 day. In
P002 L015: a next step, this proposal is simpli ﬁed into a design value for the
P002 L016: temperature ratio:

P002 L020: with qs;min (/C14 C) the minimum indoor surface temperature and qi
P002 L021: and qe respectively the inside and outside temperature ( /C14 C). A
P002 L022: temperature ratio of 0.7 is proposed as criterion, related to an
P002 L023: acceptable mould risk of 5%. A lower ratio introduces an unac-
P002 L024: ceptable high mould risk.
P002 L025: Remarks: Although the temperature ratio is often used as a design
P002 L026: criterion, an in situ study on 35 mould infested dwellings [1 7]indi-
P002 L027: cated that this criterion may not be used as a stand-alone perfor-
P002 L028: mance. In the research, 12 of the 35 dwellings required a temperature
P002 L029: ratio higher than 0.7 to exclude mould growth. Though, this growth
P002 L030: could for the 12 cases be addressed to a too low ventilation rate, rain
P002 L031: inﬁltration, less heating or a too high U-value behind cupboards. It
P002 L032: can be concluded that the criterion based on the temperature ratio is
P002 L033: a rudimentary criterion, taking the humiditye the main inﬂuencing
P002 L034: parameter e only indirectly into account. Besides, the design value is
P002 L035: deﬁned for Belgian climatic data. For other climates another design
P002 L036: value could be preferable. Note furthermore that a relative humidity
P002 L037: threshold of 100% in cases of 1 day exposure is questionable since
P002 L038: liquid water hinders mould development. A threshold of 99% relative
P002 L039: humidity is more plausible. The critical RH threshold of 80% is also
P002 L040: recommended in [18].
P002 L041: 2.2. Time-of-wetness
P002 L042: Adan [13] performed a series of experiments to study the
P002 L043: response of the fungal volume or water content in cases of transient
P002 L044: relative humidity courses. He observed a distinct increase in the cell
P002 L045: volume if the relative humidity increases, which indicates that
P002 L046: short periods of high relative humidity may not be neglected in
P002 L047: mould analyses. To indicate the water availability under transient
P002 L048: conditions, he introduced the time-of-wetness (TOW), given by:
P002 L049: TOW ¼ cyclic wet period ðRH /C21 0:8Þ
P002 L050: cyclic period ðwet þ dryÞ (2)
P002 L051: He investigated for step changes in relative humidity the effect of
P002 L052: the low RH, the TOW and the daily frequency ( f) of the high RH
P002 L053: periods for a given TOW and concluded that a TOW below 0.5 retards
P002 L054: mould substantially. A TOW above 0.5 results in a non-linear relation
P002 L055: between the TOW and the mould growth and an increased mould
P002 L056: damage. Besides, Adan developed sigmoid curves, which enable
P002 L057: a satisfactory ﬁt of the mould growth rate (in [13] deﬁned as the
P002 L058: combination of lag phase and exponential phase) of Penicillium
P002 L059: chrysogenum on gypsum board material. In this, the average rating is
P002 L060: deﬁned as in the BS3900 standard (see Table 1).
P002 L061: Remarks: Due to the limited investigated substrate types and
P002 L062: mould species, at the moment no general conclusions can be drawn
P002 L063: based on Adan ’s experiments. Also the sigmoid curves, developed
P002 L064: based on measured data on gypsum board materials inoculated with
P002 L065: P. chrysogenum, cannot be used to predict fungal growth in cases of
P002 L066: other substrates, species or environmental conditions. Furthermore,
P002 L067: the TOW does not include a gradation in favourable relative humidity
P002 L068: and can consequently not be used as a stand-alone performance.
P002 L069: Besides, no criterion is indicated to the TOW-value.

P006 L034: 2.7. Isopleth models

P006 L039: from unfavourable T- and RH-conditions for mould growth. The

P009 L032: 2.9. Biohygrothermal model
P009 L033: To make a more reliable prediction of the mould risk possible in
P009 L034: cases of transient conditions, Sedlbauer extended his isopleth
P009 L035: model with the biohygrothermal model [14,38,40,41]. In this model
P009 L036: the moisture balance of a spore, which has a certain osmotic
P009 L037: potential and which can consequently absorb water from the
P009 L038: environment dependent of the transient boundary conditions, is
P009 L039: calculated. This means also that even an interim drying out of the
P009 L040: fungus spores can be considered. To calculate this process, the
P009 L041: spore interior is characterised by a moisture retention curve and
P009 L042: the spore wall by a humidity-dependent sd-value. The spore is
P009 L043: supposed to have germinated when a certain moisture content e
P009 L044: the critical moisture content e is reached. From this point on
P009 L045: metabolic processes and mould growth can start. The critical
P009 L046: moisture content can be determined based on the moisture
P009 L047: retention curve of the spore and the critical relative humidity found
P009 L048: in the germination LIM-curve. The type of substrate can be taken
P009 L049: into account by using the substrate dependent isopleth system in
P009 L050: the determination of the critical moisture content.
P009 L051: By use of this model, the required time till germination and the
P009 L052: mould growth (mm/day) can be determined. The material prop-
P009 L053: erties of the model spore are developed based on properties of
P009 L054: bacteria which are slightly adapted. This adaptation is performed
P009 L055: by ﬁtting the material properties in this way that the calculated
P009 L056: germination time equals the measured germination time in cases of
P009 L057: constant conditions (which can be found in the isopleths).
P009 L058: Remarks: Although the simpli ﬁcation of the mould growth into
P009 L059: a model spore with moisture properties makes a HAM-approach
P009 L060: possible, care should be taken with the simpli ﬁcation of the
P009 L061: rather complex mould growth process. The development of the
P009 L062: spore properties is furthermore questionable due to the large bio-
P009 L063: logical difference between moulds and bacteria. Moreover, the
P009 L064: mould species dependent isopleths developed by, e.g. Ayerst, Smith
P009 L065: and Hill, Clarke and Rowan and Sedlbauer suggest a species
P009 L066: dependent behaviour which is not included in the model spore.
P009 L067: In the biohygrothermal model an initial relative humidity is
P009 L068: given to the spore. In WUFI-Bio the default value for this relative
P009 L069: humidity is set at 50%. Though, according to WUFI-Bio [42], this

P011 L002: To investigate the differences between the existing mould
P011 L003: prediction models, ﬁve ﬁctitious stepwise relative humidity and
P011 L004: temperature conditions ( Table 9 ) are assumed. The analysis is
P011 L005: subdivided into three steps: the investigation of (1) the mould
P011 L006: intensity in function of time, (2) the predicted mould risk in
P011 L007: combination with the time after which a potential risk starts and
P011 L008: (3) the total mould growth intensity after one year. Note however
P011 L009: that the scope of the mould prediction models is to evaluate the
P011 L010: mould risk and not to determine the exact mould growth intensity
P011 L011: or the starting point. Therefore, the obtained start times and
P011 L012: intensities may not be interpreted too precisely and are only used
P011 L013: to analyse the working principle of the different models and its
P011 L014: potential impact on the mould risk conclusions.

P011 L016: model and the WUFI-Bio mould index the WUFI-Bio software
P011 L017: [42] is used. For the initial spore relative humidity the default

P011 L020: both models, substrate category I is assumed. In the original VTT
P011 L021: model pine wood is assumed as substrate ( W ¼ 0, SQ ¼ 0), while

P013 L050: 4. Conclusion
P013 L051: Since mould growth can have an impact on the indoor air quality
P013 L052: and the occupant ’s health and since it can introduce economical
P013 L053: and social damage, attention should be paid to mould growth
P013 L054: prevention. To predict the mould growth risk one of the several
P013 L055: mould prediction models found in literature can be used. These
P013 L056: models can be subdivided in rather basic prediction parameters
P013 L057: (time-of-wetness, Johansson ’s indices) and more in depth deter-
P013 L058: ministic models (VTT model, isopleth systems, biohygrothermal
P013 L059: model). However, basic models as well as more advanced mould
P013 L060: models make a simpli ﬁcation of the very complex mould process.
P013 L061: Consequently, care should be taken when analysing the results.
P013 L062: In a ﬁrst part of this paper an overview of the different existing
P013 L063: mould prediction models was given. It was shown that, although
P013 L064: a lot of research has already been carried out to come to a more
P013 L065: accurate and reliable prediction, still some shortcomings, physically
P013 L066: unrealistic phenomena, contradictions, etc. can be found in the
P013 L067: current available models.
P013 L068: In a second part, it was shown that, due to simpli ﬁcations or
P013 L069: assumptions made in the different models, a different conclusion
P013 L070: may be drawn in the analysis depending on the model used. To
P013 L071: investigate the effect of the different models the mould risk, the start
P013 L072: of the germination process and the mould intensity were predicted
P013 L073: for ﬁctitious
P013 L074: conditions. It was shown that for some conditions no
P013 L075: mould risk is predicted with the VTT model whereas according to
P013 L076: the other models mould risk exists. Furthermore, a large spread was
P013 L077: found for the germination times obtained with the different models.
P013 L078: The largest germination times were found for the mould indices
P013 L079: (VTT, WUFI-Bio), which can be due to the germination criterion used
P013 L080: in this model ( M /C21 1). Also for the mould intensity some contra-
P013 L081: dictions are found between the models. For example, in cases of
P013 L082: cycles of 10 h 58% RH followed by 20 h 97% RH and a temperature of
P013 L083: 20
P013 L084: /C14 C an original and sensitive VTT mould index lower than 1 were
P013 L085: obtained, whereas the WUFI-Bio mould index reached a value of 6.
P013 L086: The course of the WUFI-Bio mould index in function of time was
P013 L087: furthermore shown to differ also from the VTT mould index for the
P013 L088: other conditions. Apparently, the BET-function given by Krus et al.
P013 L089: [43] seems not reliable for every condition. Besides, no decline
P013 L090: during unfavourable periods was found in the WUFI-Bio mould
P013 L091: index. Latter absence could also be noticed in the biohygrothermal
P013 L092: model and Moon ’s germination graph method. Consequently, also
P013 L093: for these models a large mould growth was predicted. When
